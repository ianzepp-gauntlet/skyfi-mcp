# Title Slide — "SKYFI MCP"

Create a wide-format (16:9) title slide for a technical presentation. The background is a view of Earth from low Earth orbit — the planet's curved horizon fills the lower third of the frame, with city lights or terrain texture visible below and the black of space above. This should feel like an actual satellite downlink camera view — not a glossy stock photo. Slightly desaturated, maybe a faint scan-line or sensor-noise texture overlaid to give it a raw telemetry feel.

**Center-left, large text:** "SKYFI MCP" in white monospace bold. Below it in smaller amber/gold monospace: "Ian Zepp". Below that, even smaller, in a muted white or gray: "March 2026".

**Right side, running vertically from top to bottom:** A thin tracking line (like a satellite ground track or a vertical timeline) with seven waypoint dots spaced evenly along it. Each dot has a short label extending to the left in small monospace text:
- Dot 1 (top): "ARCHITECTURE"
- Dot 2: "CONFIRMATION GATE"
- Dot 3: "EVAL HARNESS"
- Dot 4: "COVERAGE"
- Dot 5: "CLI"
- Dot 6: "CORRIDOR PROBLEM"
- Dot 7 (bottom): "CORRIDOR CHUNKING"

The tracking line should be thin, amber or white, with small filled circles at each waypoint. It should feel like a flight plan or an orbital pass schedule — not a corporate bullet list.

**Overall style:** Dark, cinematic, technical. The Earth view provides the atmosphere but doesn't dominate — the text and tracking line are the focal elements. No logos, no gradients, no decorative borders. The mood is: this is a serious satellite infrastructure presentation, not a pitch deck.
